Bee a Hero

Save the Hive, Save the World!



❃   Over 25% of bee species are endangered. Wild bees, like bumblebees and solitary bees, lack the protections often given to honeybees.


❃   Urban development, monoculture farming, and deforestation reduce the habitats bees need to survive.


❃   Climate change alters blooming patterns, leading to food shortages during key foraging times.


❃   Neonicotinoids are a class of insecticides that target insects' nervous systems and are linked to pollinator declines.


❃   Neonicotinoids and other pesticides weaken bees' immune systems, disrupt navigation, and lower reproduction.


❃   Population declines reduce genetic diversity, making bees more vulnerable to environmental stress and disease.


❃   Wild bees are more vulnerable due to the lack of management and treatment they receive compared to honey bees.


❃   Monocultures offer limited and short-lived food sources, contributing to poor bee nutrition.


❃   Varroa mites and Nosema infections spread rapidly, especially where wild and managed bees overlap.


❃   Bees pollinate over 75% of food crops, making them vital for agriculture and global food security.


❃   Species in danger include: Rusty Patched Bumble Bee (USA), Franklins Bumble Bee (USA), Patagonian Bumble Bee (South America), and Yellow-Banded Bumble Bee (Canada/US).


❃   Light pollution interferes with the nocturnal activities of some bee species, disrupting their foraging patterns and reducing pollination efficiency.






Restore Native Habitats

Plant native wildflowers and restore habitats in cities, farms, and forests.





Conserve Wild Bee Populations

Promote protection and monitoring of wild bee species with dedicated conservation programs.





Pesticide Control

Reduce pesticide use and advocate for bans on harmful chemicals like neonicotinoids.





Genetically Diverse Populations

Encourage conservation breeding to maintain healthy and genetically diverse bee populations.





Planned Agricultural

Support diverse planting and staggered blooming schedules to improve bee nutrition year-round.





Disease Control

Implement better health management for bee populations to control diseases and parasites.
